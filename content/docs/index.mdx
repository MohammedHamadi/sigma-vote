# Introduction

Welcome to the **SigmaVote Documentation**. This portal provides an in-depth look at the architecture, cryptographic principles, and operational workflows of our secure electronic voting platform.

SigmaVote is designed to bridge the gap between user-friendly web experiences and the rigorous security requirements of democratic elections. By utilizing **Paillier Homomorphic Encryption**, **Non-Interactive Zero-Knowledge Proofs (NIZKPs)**, and **RSA Blind Signatures**, we provide a system where:

- **No one** knows how you voted (Privacy).
- **Everyone** can verify the count was done correctly (Verifiability).
- **Malicious actors** cannot change or invalidate votes (Integrity).

## <Shield className="inline-block mr-2 h-5 w-5 text-primary" /> Our Philosophy

We believe that for electronic voting to be trusted, it must be **Open**, **Verifiable**, and **Mathematically Sound**. SigmaVote isn't just a web app; it's a cryptographic protocol designed to protect the most fundamental unit of democracy: the individual vote.
